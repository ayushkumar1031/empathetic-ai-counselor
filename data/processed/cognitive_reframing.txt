Technique: Cognitive Reframing

Cognitive reframing is a psychological technique that helps people identify and challenge unhelpful thoughts. It is commonly used in cognitive behavioral therapy.

When to use:

* Negative self-talk
* Catastrophic thinking
* Excessive worry
* Low confidence

Process:

1. Identify the thought causing distress.
2. Ask whether the thought is supported by evidence.
3. Consider alternative explanations.
4. Replace the original thought with a more balanced one.

Example:

Original thought:
"If I fail this exam, my future is ruined."

Balanced thought:
"This exam is important, but one result does not determine my entire future."

Benefits:
Cognitive reframing does not force positive thinking. Instead, it encourages realistic and balanced thinking. Over time, it can reduce anxiety, improve resilience, and support healthier emotional responses to stressful situations.
