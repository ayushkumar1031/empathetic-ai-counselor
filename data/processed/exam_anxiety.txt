Topic: Exam Anxiety

Exam anxiety is a common reaction to academic pressure. It can cause excessive worry, difficulty concentrating, sleep disturbances, and physical symptoms such as a racing heart or stomach discomfort.

Common signs:

* Constant worry about failure
* Difficulty focusing while studying
* Avoidance of preparation
* Trouble sleeping before exams
* Negative self-talk

Helpful strategies:

1. Break study material into smaller sections.
2. Create a realistic study schedule.
3. Practice active recall and mock tests.
4. Take regular breaks.
5. Avoid comparing yourself with others.

Helpful mindset:
Feeling anxious before an exam does not mean you are unprepared. Anxiety is often a sign that something is important to you. The goal is to manage the anxiety rather than eliminate it completely.

If anxiety becomes overwhelming, combine preparation techniques with relaxation methods such as box breathing or grounding exercises.
