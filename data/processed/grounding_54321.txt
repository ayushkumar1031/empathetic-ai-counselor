Technique: 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise

The 5-4-3-2-1 technique is a grounding exercise designed to reduce anxiety and bring attention back to the present moment.

When to use:

* Anxiety
* Panic
* Racing thoughts
* Feeling overwhelmed

Steps:

Identify:

* 5 things you can see
* 4 things you can touch
* 3 things you can hear
* 2 things you can smell
* 1 thing you can taste

Purpose:
This exercise shifts attention away from distressing thoughts and toward immediate sensory experiences. It can interrupt cycles of worry and help restore a sense of control.

Benefits:
Grounding exercises are simple, require no equipment, and can be performed almost anywhere. They are particularly useful when anxiety causes attention to become focused on future threats or worst-case scenarios.

The goal is not to suppress emotions but to reconnect with the present moment and reduce emotional intensity.
