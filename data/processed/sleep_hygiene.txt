Topic: Sleep Hygiene

Sleep hygiene refers to habits and environmental factors that support consistent, high-quality sleep. Poor sleep can increase stress, reduce concentration, and worsen emotional regulation.

Recommended habits:

1. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
2. Avoid caffeine several hours before bedtime.
3. Limit screen exposure before sleep.
4. Keep the bedroom cool, dark, and quiet.
5. Use the bed primarily for sleep rather than work or study.

Common mistakes:

* Studying in bed
* Using social media late at night
* Sleeping at drastically different times each day
* Consuming large amounts of caffeine in the evening

Benefits:
Improved sleep quality can enhance memory, emotional stability, focus, and stress tolerance. Good sleep habits are often one of the most effective long-term strategies for managing anxiety and maintaining mental well-being.
